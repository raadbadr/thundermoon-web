The Studio

About ThunderMoon

One developer. One vision. Games where light meets geometry.

The story

ThunderMoon is the indie game studio of Raad Badr, a developer based in Saudi Arabia. The studio was born from a simple obsession: the visual language of light cutting through darkness.

Before ThunderMoon, there were years of building apps — parking solutions, utilities, platforms. But games were always the gravitational pull. The question wasn't if, but when.

In 2026, that question was answered with LaserBeam — a puzzle game built in Unreal Engine 5 where every level is a study in spectral physics. Split light, reflect it, merge colors, solve the geometry.

The philosophy

ThunderMoon games follow a design language called Spectral Geometry:

• Darkness is material — not absence, but canvas. Deep blue-black space that breathes.

• Light is action — laser-green for action, cyan for energy, purple for transformation. No fourth color above the dark.

• Precision over decoration — every line, every angle, every halo is deliberate. No random glow, no free fog.

• Mobile native — built for touch, designed for the device in your pocket. Not a port, not a compromise.
